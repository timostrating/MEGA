Opmerkingen

The courses "Bioenergy and Metabolism" and "Bioenergy and Bioresources" can not be both part of the student's programme of 180 ects, since the first part of these courses overlap.

Students who want to do courses in their minor that are not in this list will need to ask for approval from the Board of Examiners.
(In general, the university-level and faculty-level minors like the educational minor are approved by the Board of Examiners.)

Pay attention to your planning: Life science courses are block (blok) courses. Chemistry courses are ribbon (lint) courses.

14General Process EquipmentCHTGPE05E
General process equipment function (such as pipes, pumps, compressors, heat exchanger, tanks and vessels), type, design and operation will be elucidated during lectures in addition to the required self-study on the subjects. Weekly – personal and small group - assignment have to be executed to enhance equipment modelling and equipment design understanding. The engineering computer software Aspen will be introduced and course specific models will be used to enhance computer added equipment design and understanding. Students will during the course continuously work on assignments and two assignments have an entrée test on knowledge to be required upfront. Literature survey and engineering skills as part of the academic skills will be addressed. Nestor will be used for support during the module and modeling assistance will be available.

24MicrobiologieWLB0704
Theoretisch deel:
De prokaryote cel (bouw en functie van celbestanddelen, verschillen tussen bacteria en archaea); groei en voedingseisen van micro-organismen (groeikinetiek, nutriënten en andere milieuparameters, biofilms); energietransductie en metabolische diversiteit bij prokaryoten (glycolyse, citroenzuurcyclus, elektronentransportketens, protonmotive force, gisting, anaerobe ademhaling, chemolithotrofie, stikstoffixatie, methaanvorming); extremofiele micro-organismen; antibiotica; industriële microbiologie; interactie mens-microbe (normale flora, pathogenen, virulentiefactoren, afweer); taxonomie (klassieke en DNA-methoden).

Practicum:
Technieken om contaminatievrij te werken, fasecontrastmicroscopie, kweken van bacteriecultures (o.a. opzetten van ophopingscultures, uitstrijken op agarplaten, kweken in fermentor), microbiologische conservering, identificatie van bacteriën middels fenotypische en genotypische methoden, stofwisselingstypen bij Escherichia coli, activiteitsbepalingen aan intra- en extracellulaire enzymen, antibiotica (o.a. resistentieoverdracht d.m.v. conjugatie, gehaltebepaling).

25Moleculaire biologie & medische biologieWLB07018
Cellen vormen in onderlinge samenhang en samenspraak functionele weefsels, organen en organismen. In deze cursus zullen basale genexpressie processen worden uitgelegd en wordt er ingegaan op de ontwikkeling tot multicellulaire organismen en apoptosis, met focus op de neuronale ontwikkeling. Tevens zullen recente ontwikkelingen met betrekking tot de rol van stamcellen aan de orde komen en wordt de rol van stamcellen in de instandhouding van verschillende weefsels behandeld. Aangezien ziekten vaak worden veroorzaakt door storingen in deze complexe mechanismen, is de behandelde stof cruciaal voor een goed begrip van gezondheid en ziekte.
Als laatste zal er worden ingegaan op infecties van cellen door bacteriën en virussen - infectieroutes, respons van de gastheercel en de rol van het immuunsysteem in het herkennen en verwijderen van pathogenen.

47Science, Ethics, Technology, and SocietyCHBBSCSO
The course allows the students to develop a critical and responsible attitude towards their own role as a scientist and the
consequences of their actions in a broader societal context. Students will be engaged into thinking about scientific and innovative
practices that can address the global challenges of our time in a collaborative, ethical and sustainable ways. To understand the
relations between science and society a number of basic elements from ethics, philosophy of science, argumentation, innovation,
and policy making will be introduced. During various assignments students will practice applying these elements in (the assessment
of) debates, controversies and cases related to the large societal challenges of our time, including themes such as responsible
research and innovation, energy and sustainability, and the role of science and engineering in it. In a final essay, students will apply
what they have learned by assessing and reporting on the science and society relationship in a specific case.
