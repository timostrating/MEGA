4Cultural Leadership IILCR008M05
The main goal is to help students developing a vision on cultural leadership, as well as to identify such visions (and accompanying values), especially where they tend to remain implicit (as in policy documents or in mission statements).

1. The first goal is to discuss the difference as well as the complex relation between science (theory, observation) and ideology / politics, between hypotheses and facts on the one hand and values and opinions on the other.
2. We will then discuss issues / documents (policy statements, mission statements, interviews) issued by different actors in the field of culture, in order to identify the implicitly or explicitly stated vision and values. Combining an historical dimension with contemporary issues (actuality), we will relate these to value systems and ideologies. We systematically compare different ideological perspectives in terms of value-regimes.
3. Students are then encouraged to develop and build an argument for their own vision on cultural leadership.

7Culture and LawLCR002M05
Participants in this course will be presented first with a general introduction to law. This will take place on the basis of both a legal as well as a philosophical perspective. The course starts with a general overview of the main branches of law, i.e. private law, administrative law, criminal law and constitutional law. The characteristics and key concepts of each of these areas of law will be analyzed and discussed. The general introduction to law will be rounded off with a philosophical analysis of the concept of law. Fundamental philosophical questions relating to law will be addressed, such as: ‘What is a rule?’ and ‘How does a legal rule differ from other types of rules?’

In addition to the general introduction to law, certain aspects will be addressed that are of particular importance to cultural policy-making. In relation to this, the following themes will be discussed:

-Government policy in relation to cultural affairs is often based upon the assumption that law can be used as a means to realize policy aims. This assumption, however, is problematic. In this course, attention will be given to the question as to what extent the legal system is capable of steering society into a specific desired direction. In relation to this, theories shall be discussed concerning the idea of law as a self-regulating autonomous system.

- Most cultural associations are constituted on the basis of legal constructs derived from private law. However, many of these associations receive public funding. Therefore, these associations are to a certain extent hybrid mixes combining both private law as well as public law elements. In this course, special attention will be given to the differences between private law and public law and the problems that potentially arise when private and public are mixed together.

10Research DesignLWR014M05
Students work under the supervision of a senior researcher (i.e. the course coordinator) on the following topics:

a. Selecting, studying and discussing key texts related to the master thesis.
b. Setting up a research design (including a research question, a hypothesis and a paragraph on relevance) – based on the proposed thesis – according to generally accepted academic standards, the results of which are aimed at an academic audience.
c. Discuss this design with fellow students on the basis of a fixed set of qualitative criteria and adjust the proposal where necessary.
