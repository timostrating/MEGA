Toon korte vakomschrijvingen

1Master WorkHCHTMW0105
The masterwork is the final project where you have to demonstrate that you can put the knowledge and skills you have learned in the HTSM honours master track into practice by designing and implementing your own project. This involves independently (or in small teams) designing and carrying out your complete project incorporating specific knowledge about High Tech Systems and Materials. All the stages of implementing this project, from formulating objectives to writing a plan of approach to pursuing and presenting the final result, have to be completed by the student.
The content of the project can be geared to your plans for the future. Do you want to start your own business when you graduate? Do you want to organize conferences, or would you prefer to work on a research project? All these are possible subjects for a Master Work.
The scientific coordinator will assess whether the proposed project is a suitable tool for testing if the student can successfully apply the acquired knowledge and skills in the field of leadership and if this project meets the requirements.
The topic and design of the Masterwork must reflect the student’s ability to achieve the learning outcomes of the Masterwork.

2MasterclassesHCHTMC0105
The masterclasses will develop and complement your knowledge and understanding of various aspects of the HTSM sector, such as your own field, other disciplines, product development, innovation and engineering. These masterclasses enable you and your team to acquire the necessary skills for preparing and conducting your team’s product development assignment.
Masterclasses on Demand will be offered depending on the preference of the participating students.

3Personal DevelopmentHCHTPD0102
The HTSM Master programme offers personal development modules aimed at teaching their students a wide variety of skills, which are useful both for the completion of the HTSM-track as well as outside of the programme. These modules help you grow and develop your ability to plan & create actions towards specific goals.
To name a few examples, the programme features a class on brainstorming and workshops on giving and receiving feedback, in which you can learn to give feedback to your peers in a way that is both pleasant and effective.
Personal development modules on demand will be offered depending on the preference of the participating students.

4Product ProposalHCHTPP0103
Students are required to write a Product Proposal in which they pitch an improvement to an existing product. These proposals are written in interdisciplinary groups. The Product Proposal has to cover 1) the technological options for improvement (i.e. show that it is possible, and what the effects will be), 2) the commercial demand for this improvement (i.e. show that people would buy this, so a company has a reason to invest in it), and 3) a financial plan for the development of the improvement including a thorough business model.
Secondly, each group has to write a Plan of Approach which should demonstrate how they want to improve the product (i.e. time table, which resources and facilities are required, etc.).
Students have to present their Product Proposal and Plan of Approach to a panel of relevant experts. This presentation will be judged on the way in which it clearly conveys the main points of the proposal, the way in which the students convince the panel of experts to implement their ideas, as well as the way in which they handle questions from the panel of experts regarding their proposal.

5Summer SchoolHCHTSS0105
Your team will manufacture, test and finalize the proposed and approved solution during a Summer School on site at the labs of the respective industrial host. To this end the necessary facilities will be placed at your disposal. At the end of the summer school, students have to present the results of their product improvement to a panel of experts.
