3Social Complexity: CharacterisationHCUC10105
We try to get to a characterization of social complexity. The course introduces students to complexity studies as a preparation of courses investigating the way agents are to behave in a complex environment.
As such, homo agens replaces homo economicus. What is involved? How does it affect our decision making space? What does it imply for human action and human interaction. How do we have to think ‘differently’ in an undetermined, non-linear and complex world?
FaculteitHonours College
VoertaalEngels
Docent(en)
OnderwijsvormBijeenkomst(en), Discussiebijeenkomsten, Seminaar
ToetsvormEssay, Portfolio+
ECTS5
EntreevoorwaardenOpen only to UCG students.
Only available for students who have been accepted into the BA Honours College Programme.
OpmerkingenThis course is an organic part of a three course – two projects structure that runs over the entire bachelor. Students are required to participate intensively and to engage in a very high level of self-organisation. They are expected, for instance, to pro-actively suggest and invite guest speakers and develop a personal learning path, laid out in a portfolio. Already in this course the students are asked to dig up additional texts about social complexity for the next course. Despite the high degree of independent learning demanded, this will all happen under the supervision of the course coordinator.

The following condition (Article 3.6 of the EAR, to be found in your Student Portal under Organizations) applies: “Except in the event of extraordinary circumstances, all Honours Programme course units have a 100% attendance requirement, which includes preparation for and active participation in the course units.”
